How It Works

From Raw Data to Fraud Detection

Step 1: Data Exploration

Analyze the imbalanced dataset with 284,807 transactions where only 0.17% are fraudulent. Understand the severe class imbalance challenge.

Step 2: Sampling Techniques

Apply three sampling methods: undersampling, oversampling, and SMOTE to balance the dataset while preserving important patterns.

Step 3: Model Training

Train Random Forest and XGBoost models on different sampling strategies to find the optimal combination for fraud detection.

Step 4: Evaluation & Optimization

Evaluate models using precision, recall, ROC-AUC, and perform cost-benefit analysis to determine the best decision threshold.

The Challenge: Class Imbalance

Traditional accuracy metrics are misleading when dealing with rare events like credit card fraud. A naive model that predicts all transactions as legitimate achieves 99.83% accuracy but catches 0% fraud!

Fraud Detection Challenge

Our Solution: SMOTE + Ensemble Models

We use Synthetic Minority Over-sampling Technique (SMOTE) to create realistic fraud samples, combined with XGBoost for superior performance in detecting fraudulent patterns.

Precision vs Recall Tradeoff

The key insight: fraud detection involves balancing the cost of missing fraud ($100 per transaction) against the cost of false alarms ($5 per transaction).

Precision-Recall Curves

High Recall (Catch More Fraud)

Lower thresholds catch 98% of fraud but generate many false positives. Best when fraud losses significantly exceed false positive costs.

Balanced Approach

Optimal threshold (0.9) catches 38% of fraud with manageable false positives. Minimizes total business cost at $8,545.

High Precision (Fewer False Alarms)

Higher thresholds catch fewer fraud cases but with very few false positives. Best when customer friction is costly.

Key Insights

Cost Optimization

Threshold 0.9 reduces total business cost by 93% compared to 0.1 threshold while still catching 38% of fraudulent transactions.

Precision vs Recall Trade-off

Higher thresholds improve precision (fewer false positives) but reduce recall (more fraud missed). Business context determines optimal balance.

Model Reliability

XGBoost + SMOTE provides consistent performance across different evaluation metrics, making it suitable for production deployment.

Business Impact

At optimal threshold, the system prevents $18,400 in fraud losses while only incurring $2,445 in false positive costs.
